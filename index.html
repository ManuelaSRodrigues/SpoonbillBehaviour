Where do juvenile spoonbills carry out their daily activities?

The maps provided here were produced in the study “The functional role of specific habitats for the daily routine of juvenile spoonbills in protected coastal wetlands” (https://doi.org/10.1007/s12237-025-01598-x). Data presented here originates from 44 juvenile Eurasian spoonbills (Platalea leucorodia) tagged with solar-powered GPS/GSM device containing a 3 axial accelerometer sensor in two Portuguese wetlands, Tagus Estuary and Ria Formosa. We studied their habitat use during their first year of life, using accelerometers (and its calibration; see paper for details) to distinguish the behaviour of the tagged birds.

Here we present habitat usage as heatmaps for foraging and non- foraging activities (preening and roosting).
